Camera#

Exposure Triangle#

  • Aperture f/<num>: Controls the amount of light entering the lens. The smaller the <num>, the wider the aperture, resulting in a shallower depth of field and more background blur (bokeh).
  • Shutter Speed 1/<num>: The smaller the <num>, the longer the exposure time, making moving objects appear more blurred.
  • ISO ISO <num>: Light sensitivity. The larger the <num>, the higher the ISO, resulting in a brighter photo but rougher image quality (more noise/grain).

Focal Length#

The larger the focal length, the more it resembles a telescope (magnification), and the shallower the depth of field.

White Balance#

Used to restore color temperature.

  • Cloudy scenes tend to appear bluish.
  • Indoor portraits tend to appear warm.

Exposure Compensation#

Used to restore brightness and darkness.

  • Increase compensation for backlit portraits.
  • Increase compensation for white objects to brighten them.
  • Decrease compensation for black objects to restore texture.
